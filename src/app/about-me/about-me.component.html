Engineering for the Experience
I’m a Full Stack Engineer at Stanford on the ClinGen team. We help researchers curate genes and diseases for use in precise medicine.

I enjoy taking ownership of engineering both backend and frontend to create product that impacts people’s daily lives, and I'm enthusiastic about user-centric methodology. How can we engineer a better experience? Users want prompt and constant feedback from the system they use, which creates new challenges in the UI and backend system.

My background in Computer Science and Human-Computer Interaction helps me understand users needs, and specialize in building systems that scale to meet performance goals and deliver a smooth experience.
Empathy
Empathy brings us all together. To me, engineering a great user experience is a journey to empathy. Putting myself into others shoes. Being aware that everyone has a bias, acknowledging that, getting out of my developer bias, and try to view in other's lens.

It also means engineering a great developer experience. I care about the code I write, how it could be maintained by other team members, and less prone to error.

We're all in this together.
